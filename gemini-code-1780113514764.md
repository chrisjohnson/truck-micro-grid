## 1. System Overview & Architecture
This system is a dual-battery, solar-assisted 12V DC micro-grid split between a truck engine bay and a truck bed control panel. The system architecture relies on an interlocking control logic loop to manage multi-source charging safely across long distances while maintaining strict wire and component safety thresholds.

### Key Performance Constraints:
* **Interlocking Logic:** Charging paths are mutually exclusive; the DC-DC charger and the voltage-sensing combiner cannot engage simultaneously.
* **Packaging Optimization:** Symmetrical 1-to-1 power distribution on a 5-position MIDI fuse block, skipping the large unfused main supply stud.
* **Connection Infrastructure:** Standardized on an Amass XT60 flush-port connection ecosystem with integrated dust covers for modular battery box removal.

## 2. Core Hardware Inventory & Specifications

### Power Sources & Buffers:
* **House Bank:** 2x Goldenmate 100Ah Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4) batteries.
  * *Internal Protection:* Integrated BMS with Low-Voltage Disconnect (LVD) threshold $\le 11.0\text{V}$.
  * *Local Fusing:* Each battery box is independently fused directly at the box at **30A**.
  * *Pigtails:* 12 AWG pure copper transitioning to chassis-mounted flush XT60 ports.
* **Starter Bank:** Truck primary lead-acid starting battery network.
* **Solar Array:** 1x Renogy 200W Shadowflux High-Efficiency N-Type Panel.
  * *Max Voltage ($V_{mp}$):* $31.3\text{V}$
  * *Open-Circuit Max Voltage ($V_{oc}$):* $36.5\text{V}$
  * *Max Current ($I_{sc}$):* $\sim7\text{A}$

### Management & Control Electronics:
* **DC-DC Charger:** Victron Orion-Tr Smart 12/12-18A (18A continuous output).
* **Battery Combiner:** Cyrix-Li-ct Intelligent Voltage-Sensing Combiner (bridges at $\ge 13.4\text{V}$).
* **Solar Charge Controller:** Victron SmartSolar MPPT (Mounted on the **truck-side** bus bar).
  * *Charging Priority:* Prioritizes starting batteries first; bridges to house bank via the Cyrix combiner when solar voltage pushes the truck bus bar past $13.4\text{V}$.
* **Logic Controller:** 5-Pin SPDT Automotive Relay (12V, 30/40A rated).

## 3. Interlocking Control Logic Loop (5-Pin Relay)

To prevent simultaneous operation of the Orion DC-DC and the Cyrix combiner, a 5-pin relay creates an interlocking control loop driven by the truck's ignition state.

### 🎛️ Relay Pinout & Wiring Schedule
All logic lines utilize **16 AWG or 18 AWG Oxygen-Free Copper (OFC) GXL/TXL automotive primary wire** for vibration resistance and mechanical stability.

* **Pin 30 (Common Input Power):** 16 AWG Red wire leading to **Truck Bus Bar 2**. Must be protected by a **2A inline fuse** placed physically at the bus bar stud.
* **Pin 86 (Relay Coil Positive +):** 16 AWG Blue/Yellow wire tapped into the **Ford Factory Ignition Wire** (hot only when key is in ON/RUN position).
* **Pin 85 (Relay Coil Ground -):** 16 AWG Black wire leading directly to the panel's **Common Negative Bus Bar**.
* **Pin 87a (Normally Closed - NC Output):** 16 AWG Green/Orange wire leading to **Terminal 85 (Spade)** on the **Cyrix-Li-ct Combiner**.
* **Pin 87 (Normally Open - NO Output):** 16 AWG Purple/White wire leading to the green **Remote H-Pin Terminal Block** on the **Victron Orion DC-DC Charger**.

### 🔄 Operational Truth Table
| Ignition State | Relay Coil | Pin 30 Connects To | Cyrix Combiner State | Orion DC-DC State | System Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Key OFF** | De-energized | **Pin 87a (NC)** | **Armed / Active** | **Forced OFF** | Solar panel charges truck battery; when truck hits $>13.4\text{V}$, Cyrix bridges to maintain house batteries. |
| **Key ON** | Energized | **Pin 87 (NO)** | **Forced OPEN** | **Armed / Active** | Charging bridge is mechanically broken. Orion wakes up and outputs a clean, stable 18A to house bank via the alternator highway. |

## 4. Main House Panel Power Distribution & Fusing

The house panel utilizes a 5-position MIDI / AMI bolt-down fuse block. The large, unfused input stud is bypassed entirely to create a neat, highly isolated 1:1 layout utilizing the block's internal linked copper bus plate.

```
                  [ INTERNAL LINKED COPPER BUS PLATE ]
     _______________________/_______|_____________________________________
    |               |               |               |               |
[ STUD 1 ]      [ STUD 2 ]      [ STUD 3 ]      [ STUD 4 ]      [ STUD 5 ]
  (30A)           (30A)           (30A)           (20A)           (20A)
    |               |               |               |               |
Combined       XT60 House      XT60 House       XT60 Load       XT60 Load
Orion/Cyrix     Battery 1       Battery 2        Port 1          Port 2
```

### 🎛️ 5-Fuse House Board Configuration
* **Stud 1: Combined Orion + Cyrix Highway**
  * *Fuse:* **30A MIDI**
  * *Wiring:* 8 AWG pure copper jumper connects Orion Positive Out (+) to the Cyrix House Battery Stud. A single 8 AWG wire runs from this Cyrix stud down to Fused Stud 1.
  * *Logic:* Self-fusing design. The 30A MIDI fuse at the bus bar protects the entire shared 8 AWG branch from house-bank backfeeding shorts.
* **Stud 2: XT60 Battery Box 1 Connection**
  * *Fuse:* **30A MIDI**
  * *Wiring:* 12 AWG pure copper from port to stud.
* **Stud 3: XT60 Battery Box 2 Connection**
  * *Fuse:* **30A MIDI**
  * *Wiring:* 12 AWG pure copper from port to stud.
  * *Safety Note:* Cross-battery isolation is enforced because inter-battery current must pass through *both* Stud 2 and Stud 3 MIDI fuses to cross-feed.
* **Stud 4: XT60 Load Port 1**
  * *Fuse:* **20A MIDI**
  * *Wiring:* 12 AWG pure copper.
* **Stud 5: XT60 Load Port 2**
  * *Fuse:* **20A MIDI**
  * *Wiring:* 12 AWG pure copper.

### 🔌 Load Characteristics & Appliance Protection
Standardizing load ports to **20A MIDI fuses** accommodates 100% of camp load requirements safely without altering factory appliance lines:
* *12V Compressor Fridge:* Pulls 4–5A transient spike on startup; 1–2A continuous running.
* *Diesel Heater:* Pulls 8–10A transient glow-plug ignition cycle (5 mins); 1–3A continuous running.
* *High-Output USB-C PD Charger:* Pulls $\sim8.5\text{A}$ max at full 100W laptop delivery.
* *Safety Profile:* The 20A threshold acts as a high-speed universal safety net. In the event of an appliance cord short-circuit, a 20A fuse pops fast enough to protect thin 16 AWG/18 AWG factory lines before insulation thermal breakdown occurs.

## 5. System Physics & Historical Calculations

### The 50-Foot Circuit Loop Impedance
The main charging run between the engine bay and truck bed consists of a **50-foot round-trip run of 8 AWG Copper-Clad Aluminum (CCA) wire**. 
* Total calculated loop resistance: **$\sim0.09\ \Omega$** (inclusive of 12 AWG copper pigtails, fuses, and combiner contact resistances).
* *Current Limiting Behavior:* Due to this physical line resistance, Ohm's Law chokes the maximum current that can transfer between the starter and house batteries during unmanaged balancing loops:
  $$\Delta V = 30\text{A} \times 0.09\ \Omega = 2.7\text{V}$$
* *Conclusion:* To draw a fuse-blowing 30A from the truck bank during a morning solar-driven Cyrix bridge event, the house bank voltage would have to sit below $10.7\text{V}$ ($13.4\text{V} - 2.7\text{V} = 10.7\text{V}$). Because the Goldenmate internal BMS activates its low-voltage cut-off at $\le11.0\text{V}$, **nuisance fuse-blowing from battery-to-battery balancing inrushes is physically impossible.**

### Operational Battery Box Disconnection Routine
To isolate a battery box without creating high-current DC arcs or leaving an open port energized behind a dust cap:
1. Flip the localized **In-Bed Solar Toggle Switch to OFF**. This breaks the VE.Direct loop, commanding the MPPT output to drop to 0A immediately.
2. The truck bus bar voltage stabilizes to resting ($12.6\text{V}$), causing the Cyrix combiner to instantly click **OPEN**.
3. The XT60 port is now completely dead. Safely pull the pigtail jumper and seat the integrated rubber dust cover.
4. **Optional:** Flip the In-Bed Solar Toggle back to **ON**. The MPPT will wake up, re-charge the truck battery past $13.4\text{V}$, and trigger the Cyrix to close. The house bus bar will become live behind the sealed dust cover, allowing solar panel maintenance of the vehicle's starter batteries during long-term storage while house loads (like a fridge left on the panel) run seamlessly off the vehicle grid using the starting battery as a buffer.

## 6. Localized Electronic Tweaks (Daytime Solar Pressure LED)

A customized daytime LED indicator is wired in **parallel across the PV Input lines** (PV+ to PV-) at the MPPT terminal block to visually confirm solar panel generation.

### Polarity & Structural Constraints:
* **True LED Polarity:** **Red Wire is Positive (+)**; **Yellow Wire is Negative (-)**.
* **Manufacturer Anomaly:** The pre-wired 12/24V LED features its factory current-limiting resistor bump ($\sim1\text{ k}\Omega$) attached to the **Yellow (Negative) leg**.
* **High-Voltage Adaptation:** Because the Renogy Shadowflux panel pushes up to $36.5\text{V}$, a standalone 12/24V dual-rated LED will overheat and fail. 

### The Stacked Resistor Modification:
To keep the circuit safe and manage thermal dissipation under full sun, a **1.5k$\Omega$ (1/4-Watt) resistor** must be soldered inline with the **Red (Positive) wire**. 

```
[ PV+ Terminal ] ─── [ Your 1.5k Resistor ] ─── [ Red Wire ] ─────── ( LED + )
                                                                        
[ PV- Terminal ] ─── [ Factory Bump ] ───────── [ Yellow Wire ] ──── ( LED - )
```

* *Thermal Sharing Profile:* Total series resistance climbs to $\sim2.5\text{k}\Omega$, dropping loop current to a safe, cool-running $11\text{ mA}$ to $13\text{ mA}$. Total thermal dissipation across the new 1.5k$\Omega$ resistor drops to **$0.18\text{W}$**, sitting safely within its 0.25W max rating.
* *Visual Output:* Provides distinct, solid daytime visibility under the shade of a camper shell cap without night-vision distraction (as the circuit naturally goes completely dark at sunset).
